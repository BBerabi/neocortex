This playbook provides guidance for examiners on how to handle common scenarios that might lead to the rejection, suspension, or partial reimbursement of prosthodontic treatment claims.

## Scenario 1: Missing Documentation for Missing-Tooth Provision
*   **Issue**: A prosthodontic claim does not establish when the replaced teeth were lost.
*   **Action**: The claim should be suspended so the 'missing-tooth provision' can be assessed.

## Scenario 2: Early Replacement of Prosthesis Without Justification
*   **Issue**: A replacement prosthesis is claimed before the replacement interval has elapsed, without documentation confirming that the existing prosthesis is no longer serviceable.
*   **Action**: The claim is declined.

## Scenario 3: Claim Exceeding Annual Benefit Limit
*   **Issue**: A claim exceeds the member's remaining annual benefit.
*   **Action**: The claim is reimbursed up to the unused balance of the annual maximum, with the remainder declined. The claim is not rejected in full.
