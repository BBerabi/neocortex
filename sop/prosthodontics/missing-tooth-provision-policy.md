NorthRiver has a 'missing-tooth provision' that generally restricts reimbursement for teeth lost prior to a member's coverage.

## Policy
NorthRiver does not reimburse the replacement of a tooth that was already missing before the member's cover began, *unless* the member meets the following criteria:
*   Has held continuous cover for at least twelve months, **AND**
*   The replacement is documented as a first-time replacement for that missing tooth.

## Purpose
This provision is in place to prevent members from purchasing coverage specifically to fund the replacement of long-standing gaps in their dentition.

## Applicability
This provision applies to the *missing tooth* being replaced, not to the prosthesis itself. The replacement of an *existing* bridge or denture is governed by its specific replacement interval policy, not the missing-tooth provision.
