Major Restorative Treatment encompasses more substantial repairs designed to rebuild or preserve teeth that cannot be restored with simple fillings. This category primarily includes crowns and endodontic (root canal) treatment.

## Key Characteristics
*   **Common Combination**: Crowns and root canal treatments are frequently performed together, as a tooth that has undergone a root canal often requires a crown for protection.
*   **Cost**: These procedures are significantly more expensive than basic restorative work.
*   **Reimbursement**: They are reimbursed at lower percentages under entry-level products.
*   **Documentation**: They carry stricter documentation expectations.

## Manual Review Thresholds
Due to their cost, both procedures may fall within the manual-review thresholds outlined in the general handbook. Examiners should remember that the financial referral threshold applies to the total billed value of the claim, meaning a combined root-canal-and-crown claim can approach the senior-review threshold even if individual procedures would not.
