Restorative treatment becomes relevant once a tooth has been damaged by decay, fracture or wear and requires repair. Unlike preventive care, restorative procedures almost always concern a specific, identified tooth.

Basic Restorative Treatment covers the everyday repair work that most members will need at some point: fillings and routine extractions. These procedures are reimbursed at a moderate level across all products, and because they are common, they are also the procedures where duplicate billing and over-frequent claiming are most often seen.

## Reimbursement Principle
The general reimbursement principle described earlier continues to apply throughout this chapter: reimbursement is calculated on the lower of the provider's fee and NorthRiver's eligible amount, the product's reimbursement percentage is then applied, and the annual deductible is subtracted where it has not yet been met for the benefit year.
